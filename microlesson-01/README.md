Welcome! In this lesson, you'll learn how to break down a complex goal into smaller, AI-ready tasks, and organize them into a clear plan for GenAI to help with.

---

### Why augment, not automate?

Imagine you’re responsible for a monthly report, and it’s a mess. Your job isn’t to “hand it all to the AI”—it’s to **augment** your process using GenAI as a powerful assistant.

> 💡 **Augmenting** means working *with* AI to get the best out of both human and machine.

**Quick reflection:**  
What’s the difference between “augmenting” and “automating” with AI? Jot down one idea.

## Scenario: The Messy Monthly Report

You’re a data professional supporting the sales team. At the end of each month, your manager sends you a “monthly sales report”—but it’s a mess. This month’s report is no different.

You receive a [spreadsheet](./assets/messy_monthly_report.csv) with:
- Columns and rows that aren’t consistent from week to week.
- Headers that sometimes change or are missing.
- Extra comments or notes mixed right into the data.
- Some numbers look off or don’t match totals at the bottom.
- Narrative summary text and “reminders to self” scattered in the file.
- A request in the email:  
  *“Can you pull out key insights, spot any anomalies, and draft a quick summary for leadership? I’m slammed—use whatever tools you need.”*

**Your task:**  
Augment your process using GenAI.  
But first, break down the messy report into subtasks.  
Then, design a multi-step workflow and see how well GenAI can help with each step.

> 💡 **Note:** You’re not just “cleaning up” the file... you’re making sense of it for someone else, using both your skills and GenAI as a partner.

---

## Step 1: Decompose the goal

Big goals aren’t “one-shot” problems for AI.  
First, break your goal into a set of smaller tasks.

- What do you want to achieve?
- What are the main pieces or subtasks?
- What’s a logical order?

*Group activity:*  
With your team, fill out a [Workflow Planning Template](./assets/Workflow-Planning-Template.md) for the scenario.

> 🧠 Think about extraction, transformation, analysis, summarization, formatting, or error checking.

---

## Step 2: Plan your multi-step GenAI workflow

Now, organize your subtasks into a clear, step-by-step workflow for GenAI.

1. **Taxonomy:** What type of task is each step?  
2. **Economy:** Can you reuse AI outputs between steps?  
3. **Clarity:** Are your instructions clear and specific?

*Draw your workflow—use sticky notes, a whiteboard, or a digital tool.  
Each step: Write exactly what you’d ask the AI, and what you expect in return.*

> ♻ **Tip:** The more specific you are, the better your results will be.

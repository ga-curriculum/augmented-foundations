**Learning objective:** Design multi-step prompting sequences using a consistent structure that includes taxonomy (task classification), economy (efficient reuse of inputs/outputs), and clarity (logical flow).

Now that you’ve planned your multi-step GenAI workflow, it’s time to put it into action.

---

## Step 3: Test your workflow with GenAI

- As a group, run each step of your workflow using ChatGPT (or similar).
- Did the AI do what you expected?  
- Where did it shine? Where did it get confused or make mistakes?

> ⚠ **Be ready for surprises.** GenAI models have strengths and weaknesses—this is your chance to see both!

*Keep track of which steps worked well and which ones didn’t.*

---

## Step 4: Troubleshoot and refine

Take 5 minutes as a team to review your workflow results.

- For steps that didn’t work:
    - What went wrong? (Prompt too vague? Not a GenAI strength?)
    - How might you rephrase or adjust?
- For steps that worked:
    - What made them successful?
    - Could you make them even clearer or more efficient?

> 💡 **Pause and discuss:**  
What tasks did GenAI handle best?  
Which needed human insight or context?
